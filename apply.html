🎙 Interview Scripts — Know These Cold

Mithra Life OS (2–3 min)

"I built and deployed Mithra Life OS — it's live at mithra-lifeos.com with 690 real users. It's a productivity platform where users have journals, tasks, habits, and a focus timer — all with a real-time multiplayer feature called Blend where users can work together in shared workspaces.

The technical architecture uses React on the frontend, FastAPI on the backend, and Supabase as the database. The interesting parts: user data is protected by Row-Level Security policies in PostgreSQL — every query is automatically filtered to the authenticated user's data without any manual WHERE clauses. The journal feature uses Gemini's embedding API to convert journal text into 768-dimensional vectors, stored in pgvector. Users can semantically search their past journal entries — finding similar entries even when the exact words don't match, because we're doing cosine similarity on the embeddings.

The hardest problem I solved: building the Blend multiplayer system — 6-character join codes, real-time synchronisation, and one user can host while others join. I built this in production and it handles concurrent users correctly."

DRDO Project (90 sec)

"My DRDO internship was at the Defence Research and Development Laboratory — Ministry of Defence. I built the backend infrastructure for their Defence Asset Management System. The technical challenge was reliability: defence systems cannot afford downtime, so I set up a 3-node MongoDB replica set with automatic failover under 10 seconds. If the primary node fails, the system automatically elects a new primary and continues operating without any manual intervention. The Docker Compose setup had 3 MongoDB containers plus FastAPI and nginx. The unique constraint: strict no-internet environment, so everything had to run fully air-gapped on the intranet."

RAG Questions (Phase 2+)

  • "What is RAG?" — Retrieval-Augmented Generation: instead of relying only on an LLM's internal knowledge, you retrieve relevant documents from a vector database and provide them as context. The LLM generates an answer grounded in your actual data.
  • "How does pgvector work?" — It's a PostgreSQL extension that stores and queries high-dimensional vectors. We embed text with Gemini, store the 768-dim vectors as columns, and query using cosine similarity operators to find the most semantically similar content.
  • "RAG vs fine-tuning — when do you use which?" — RAG when you have frequently-changing data you can't retrain on. Fine-tuning when you need the model to adopt a specific style or format that isn't achievable with prompting. In most production cases, RAG is faster, cheaper, and more updatable.

💰 Salary Negotiation Protocol

The Step-by-Step Negotiation Process

1
When offer arrives — buying time

"Thank you so much — I'm genuinely excited about this role and what you're building." Pause. Then: "Can I have 48 hours to review the complete offer document?" Always. Every offer. No exceptions.

2
During 48 hours — accelerate everything

Email every other active process: "I've received an offer and need to respond within 48 hours. Is there any flexibility in your timeline?" This creates urgency and often fast-tracks second-round processes.

3
Negotiating with one offer

"Based on my research on AI engineer compensation in India, and the value I'd bring with production experience in FastAPI, vector embeddings, and RAG systems, I was expecting [X+20-30% more]. Is there flexibility here?" State the number. Then stop talking. Silence is negotiation.

4
Negotiating with two offers (target situation)

"I have another offer at [X LPA] from [Company B]. I strongly prefer your company and what you're building. Can you match or get closer to [X+2L]?" This is the most powerful negotiation position possible.

5
Equity — always ask

"Can you help me understand the ESOP policy for this role? What does the vesting schedule look like and approximately what percentage would this role receive?" Even 0.1% at a Series A can be worth ₹20–50L in 3-4 years.

6
Your floor

₹12 LPA absolute minimum. Target: ₹15–18 LPA first job. Do NOT accept under ₹10 LPA for Applied AI Engineering. Your first salary anchors every subsequent negotiation for the next 3 years.

Salary Path

₹15L now → ₹28L in 18-24 months → ₹50L in 36-42 months. The path: first job with real AI ownership + 18-24 months deep production experience → switch with competing offer. Each switch with 2+ competing offers and real production experience gets 30-50% jumps. The people who reach ₹50L fast are not smarter — they negotiate every transition with competing offers and they own the AI pipeline end-to-end, not just run model inference.
